01. Philosophical Precursors

Before digital computers, Turing proposed the "Imitation Game" in 1950, setting the benchmark for machine intelligence.

The field was officially born in 1956 at the Dartmouth Workshop.

02. The Deep Learning Revolution

The shift from rule-based logic to neural networks fueled by big data and GPUs.

1997: Deep Blue

IBM's victory over Kasparov proved the power of specialized compute.

2017: Transformers

Google's "Attention is All You Need" paper revolutionized NLP.

2022: Generative AI

ChatGPT democratized AI, shifting the focus to LLMs.

03. The Agentic Turn (2025-2026)

AI has evolved from chat interfaces to autonomous agents that use "Vision-Language-Action" (VLA) models to operate in the physical world.

"By late 2025, Tesla's Optimus units were working alongside humans in factories."

04. The AGI Frontier

AGI is now defined as the "ability to figure things out"—reasoning through ambiguity and testing hypotheses. We are moving from pattern recognition to hybrid symbolic-neural architectures.

05. Infrastructure & Energy

AI is now a massive industrial project requiring 1GW data centers and nuclear energy partnerships like Microsoft's Three Mile Island deal.
