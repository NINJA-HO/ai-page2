"The historical trajectory of artificial intelligence represents a transition from philosophical inquiry into the nature of thought to the industrial-scale deployment of autonomous, reasoning agents."

By March 2026, the field has moved beyond the "Generative Era" toward "Agentic Autonomy," where AI systems are active collaborators capable of independent planning and physical manipulation.

Evolution Timeline

Pre-1950: Conceptual Foundations

Ancient automatons and Karel Čapek’s introduction of "robot" (1921). Makoto Nishimura built "Gakutensoku" in 1929.

1950-1969: The Symbolic Era

Alan Turing's "Imitation Game" (1950) and the 1956 Dartmouth Workshop where "Artificial Intelligence" was coined.

1970-1999: Winters & Expert Systems

Fluctuations in funding. Rise of rules-based "Expert Systems" like XCON (1980) and Deep Blue's victory (1997).
